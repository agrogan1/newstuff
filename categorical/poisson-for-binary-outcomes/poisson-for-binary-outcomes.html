Using Poisson Regression for Binary Outcomes

Andy Grogan-Kaylor

9 Oct 2021 12:13:25

Introduction

Logistic regression is the most frequently used model for binary outcomes. Logistic regression provides odds ratios, which while somewhat intuitive, may be misunderstood. Notably, odds ratios overstate the strength of the relationship that is implied by risk ratios.

Thus, a number of authors, including XXX have suggested that Poisson regression, which directly provides risk ratios, can be employed for binary outcomes. XXX indicate that the standard errors of the Poisson model will need to be adjusted.

This handout draws closely on the outline and presentation of ideas provided by XXX at IDRE, although the data source and variables are used are very different.
